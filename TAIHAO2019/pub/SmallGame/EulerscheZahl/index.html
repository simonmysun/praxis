We all know about $e$, the base of the natural logarithm. It can be calculated as $$e = \sum\limits_{n = 0}^{ \infty} \dfrac{1}{n!} = \frac{1}{1} + \frac{1}{1} + \frac{1}{1\cdot 2} + \frac{1}{1\cdot 2\cdot 3} + \cdots$$

Theoretically, you can find any finite or countably infinite sequence of decimal numbers continuously presented in the decimal form of $e$. Do you know where '05022000' first appears?

The answer should be the position where the first appearance starts from, in decimal form of its fractional part. e.g. '1828' appears at position 2 for the first time(additionally it appears at 6 for the second time), the answer to 1828 is '2'(without quote)
